Dwarf Planets

A dwarf planet is a mass in space that does not meet the requirements to be termed a planet, and also too big to be termed a natural satellite such as a moon. The International Astronomical Union defines a planet as being in orbit around the sun, has enough gravity to pull its mass into a rounded shape (hydrostatic equilibrium), and has cleared its orbit of other, smaller objects.

This last criterion is the point at which planets and dwarf planets differ. A planet's gravity either attracts or pushes away the smaller bodies that would otherwise intersect its orbit; the gravity of a
dwarf planet is not sufficient to make this happen.
